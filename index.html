India with a 7500km long coastline, has a rich maritime heritage. The earliest maritime evidences date back to 4500 years.
Under the visionary guidance of the Hon'ble Prime Minister of India Shri Narendra Modi Ji, the Ministry of Ports, Shipping & Waterways has envisioned to develop a world class Maritime Heritage Complex at Lothal near Ahmedabad, so that the world can experience our rich Maritime history and heritage. Lothal is one among the prominent cities of the traditional Harappan civilization dating to 2500 BCE.
At the National Maritime Heritage Complex (NMHC), we celebrate and showcase India's rich maritime heritage. This once in a lifetime experience will take the visitor through the remarkable history of maritime trade, shipbuilding prowess, cultural exchange, and technological advancements that have shaped India's maritime identity.

Our archaeological excavations have unearthed evidence of the Harappan civilization's maritime trade contacts around 4500 years ago. The Harappans were pioneers in shipbuilding, as evidenced by the remains of their dockyard in Lothal. This maritime trade not only generated significant revenue but also fuelled the transformation of a village into the first planned city in South Asia. Over the centuries, maritime connections facilitated the transfer of iron and steel technologies to South Korea and enabled dynasties like the Cholas, Pandya‚Äôs, and Marathas to develop and strengthen naval power for trade, security, and protection of maritime resources.
Beyond trade, Indian maritime contacts facilitated the diffusion of cultural, religious, and linguistic developments to Southeast and East Asia, and eventually, to the rest of the world. Buddhism, originating in India, travelled along these maritime routes, influencing China, Korea, Japan, and other Southeast Asian countries. Our maritime heritage is an integral part of India's contribution to the world's cultural tapestry.

Located in Lothal, near Ahmedabad, NMHC comprises a world class Museum with 14 theme-based galleries showcasing the rich maritime history of India. But that‚Äôs not all. Get ready to experience the state of Art Navy gallery with some stunning real life fighter jets, helicopters, Guns, and missiles. Separate arena of Coastal States pavilions, theme-based hotels, resorts, and parks, making it a world class tourism and vacation destination.
The complex also includes a replica model of Old Lothal town, surrounded by open aquatic gallery, the world's highest Lighthouse Museum, a 5D Dome Auditorium and sprawling Water bodies across the complex.

HOW TO REACH


By Road
The site is accessible from NH 47 (Ahmedabad-Rajkot Highway) which is linked to the site via SH 1 towards Dhingada village. Both these state highways connect to NH 47 further north at Bagodara.
By Train
Kalupur Railway Station (Ahmedabad) is 81 km from the site.
Bhurkhi Railway Station is 16 km from the site.
By Air
Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel International Airport (Ahmedabad) is 90 km from the site.
